## 3. Pricing Strategy

### 3.1 Value Metric

**Primary value metric: Episodes processed per month**

Why episodes, not minutes or hours:
- Podcasters think in episodes, not minutes — "I publish 4 episodes/month," not "I produce 240 minutes/month"
- Simple to understand (no credit confusion — the #1 pricing complaint across competitors)
- Scales with value: more episodes = more content generated = more value received
- Avoids the "per minute" anxiety that makes creators feel penalized for longer episodes

### 3.3 Pricing Rationale

**Free Tier ($0)**
- **Why:** 17% of podcasters spend $0. A meaningful free tier builds trust, creates word-of-mouth, and captures the long tail. Deciphr, Swell AI, and Headliner all have successful free tiers.
- **What's included:** 2 episodes/month with 3 content formats (show notes, blog, social). Enough to experience the "wow moment" but limited enough to upgrade.
- **Conversion lever:** After generating content for 2 episodes, they'll want the other 3 formats (newsletter, YouTube desc, thumbnails) and voice matching.

**Starter ($19/mo, $15 annual)**
- **Why:** Most podcasters spend under $50/mo on ALL tools. At $19/mo, we're cheaper than every direct competitor's entry tier (Castmagic $39, Capsho $29, Swell AI $29). For a weekly podcaster (4 eps/month), this is $4.75/episode.
- **Annual discount:** 21% off ($15/mo) — within the 17-20% range that maximizes annual conversion.
- **Target:** Solo podcasters publishing weekly who want all formats + voice matching.

**Pro ($49/mo, $39 annual)**
- **Why:** This is where serious podcasters and multi-show creators live. At $49/mo it's cheaper than Castmagic's Starter ($59-99/mo) while offering YouTube Channel Optimizer (which nobody else has). This is the recommended tier — the "decoy effect" makes it feel like the best value.
- **Annual discount:** 20% off ($39/mo).
- **Target:** Daily/multi-show podcasters, YouTube creators, serious content marketers.

**Agency (Custom)**
- **Why:** Agencies pay $149-300/mo at competitors. By making this "Contact us," we can price based on value and volume.
- **Target:** Podcast agencies, media companies, networks managing 5+ shows.

### 3.4 Pricing Psychology Applied

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|------------|
| **Anchoring** | Show Pro ($49) first/center on pricing page — anchors the higher value, makes Starter feel like a deal |
| **Decoy effect** | Pro at $49 with 25 episodes makes Starter at $19 with 8 episodes feel expensive per-episode ($2.38 vs $1.96), pushing upgrades to Pro |
| **Charm pricing** | $19 and $49 (not $20 and $50) — value positioning |
| **Loss aversion** | Free tier shows all 6 formats but locks 3 — they see what they're missing |
| **Social proof** | Show episode count being processed in real-time on pricing page |

### 3.5 Credit System Design

Instead of confusing minute-based credits (the #1 complaint across competitors), we use a simple episode-based system:

- 1 episode upload = 1 credit, regardless of episode length
- Credits refresh monthly (no rollover on monthly plans, rollover on annual)
- Overage: $3/episode on Starter, $2.50/episode on Pro
- All episodes include all unlocked content formats for that tier

### 3.6 Annual vs Monthly Strategy

| | Monthly | Annual | Savings |
|--|---------|--------|---------|
| Starter | $19/mo | $15/mo ($180/yr) | Save $48/yr (21%) |
| Pro | $49/mo | $39/mo ($468/yr) | Save $120/yr (20%) |
